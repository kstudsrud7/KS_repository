​Ongoing Program Responsibilities
Please read the following Program Responsibilities carefully, failure to comply with these responsibilities may result in suspension from the Program.

​
Initial that you have read and understand each responsibility: 


Must submit a copy of final class schedule to the FIGR Education Department within 10 days of receiving it each academic term. 

Must submit transcript after each academic term to the FIGR Education Department within 10 days of receiving final grades for that final term to ensure recertification for the next academic term. 

Must meet with Education Department staff at a minimum of once per academic term. Such meetings may be by phone or video conferencing, or in person. Meetings need to be scheduled ahead of time and with staff member. The purpose and agenda of each meeting will be set in advance by the Department staff and may include discussion of: academic transitions, changes to education plan, risk of failing any courses, transfer options, grade checks, review of educational goals, and steps to apply for graduation.


If your enrollment status changes (i.e. change from full-time to part-time enrollment or vise ersa) you must notify the FIGR Education Department within 10 days of the change. The Education Department may need to adjust your financial benefits, however it will take into consideration unavoidable circumstances such as a natural disaster, epidemic/pandemic, unavoidable loss of housing, or loss of immediate family member. If the amount is adjusted and you believe there is good cause to maintain the original scholarship award, you may make a written request to the Education Department to reconsider the adjustment. The FIGR Executive Director of Government Operations will issue a decision on reconsideration.

You must follow the requirements of your education plan, including any general education requirements, to successfully complete the education program. If you wish to change your educational goals, you must meet with an academic advisor, submit an updated education plan from the educational institution and meet with the FIGR Education Department within 10 days of change.

You must remain in good standing pursuant to the academic standards with the educational institution you are attending and the Program maintaining at least a 2.0 grade point average (GPA) or, if enrolled in a Master or PhD program, at least a 3.0 GPA

If you do not successfully complete courses, you must meet with an academic advisor at your educational institution and identify campus resources to assist in successful completion of the courses. Once resources have been identified, you must submit a written academic success plan to the FIGR Education Department. An academic success plan is the planned course of action developed by you and your academic advisor, with support and advice from the FIGR Education Department. The plan should detail the resources, such as tutoring or labs, that you will utilize in order to successfully complete the core course(s) at issue. The FIGR Education Department’s review of the academic success plan is required in order for you to continue participating in this Program. 

If participating in additional tutoring services other then from your institution, you must submit monthly attendance documentation showing attendance of tutoring hours. Such documentation can be in the form of attendance sheets or other verification provided by the tutoring service.
